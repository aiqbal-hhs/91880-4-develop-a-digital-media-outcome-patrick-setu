Rainbow's End

One of the best places when looking to have fun is the amusement park known as Rainbow’s End. Offering a wide variety with over twenty different rides and activities that are suitable to any and all age groups, they have a ride for everyone. Thrill seekers will find enjoyment in rides like the fearfall, stratosphere, and corkscrew coaster while those who are just looking for something less exhilarating may find rides like the log flume, dodgems, and bumper boats more enjoyable. They also have the kidz kingdom which is a designated area that is for younger children as the rides within are more toned down for their entertainment in comparison with the rest of the park. In addition to the many rides, Rainbow’s End also has food stalls in case you feel hungry, ranging from ice cream, burgers, pizzas, and more.

Rainbow’s End is located at 2 Clist Crescent, Manukau, Auckland and can be contacted through the number 09 262 2030 for extra information if needed and any questions you may have.

Skyline Rotorua

Skyline Rotorua has a range of activities that are enjoyed best with family and friends. Some of their most popular activities consist of the gondola ride which can take up to eight people when transporting from the base to the top where you can make a choice of what activity you want to do next. You could choose to race against others on the luge, ride their 400 metre long zipline through Redwood Forest while taking in the scenery, mountain bike down a track that has differing difficulties for all ability levels, or if you are looking for a more thrilling experience, their skyswing may be more appealing. With a maximum seating capacity of three people, it will lift you 50 metres above the ground and then come to a halt where a view of Lake Rotorua, the city, and further beyond can be seen. It will then be up to you to pull the release which starts the descent that can reach up to 150kph. Skyline Rotorua also has other activities such as strolling through a nature trail among 15,000 native trees and informative panels for up to an hour, playing on small diggers that are just like real excavators, and meeting a family of alpacas.

Skyline Rotorua is located at 178 Fairy Springs Road, Rotorua and can be contacted via the number 64 7 347 0027 for any questions and information needed about their place.

Escapade NZ

With six different escape room options, going to Escapade is a lot of fun with others while everyone joins together to use their minds to overcome all the tricky puzzles the room throws at them when trying to escape. Each scenario is different and unique from the other and the rooms themselves will have you feeling completely immersed in the story as you solve each problem. You could be trying to escape the space station to get away from bounty hunters hired by the space cartel, figuring out the mystery behind the words of a cryptic message Gran sent you about the beach house, or trying to get out of a locked tavern from ancient times as you race against the clock to save the city before darkness returns. Whichever scenario you want to do, the choice lies in your hands. Escapade also offers the chance to play their escape rooms even when at home. They have created an online virtual escape room experience where it is up to you to control the Game Master and solve the puzzles through Zoom.

Escapade is located on the first floor at 23 Victoria Street East, Auckland Central and can be called using their number 09 303 3570.

Skyworld

Based in the centre of Auckland, SkyWorld has quite a few sorts of entertainment. They have a very large arcade with machines of every type of popular game like racing, fighting, retro games, and many more. The GAMEON arcade also features new VR attractions in varying genres. SkyWorld also has bowling lanes to verse others at an affordable price, an 18 hole mini golf course with moving dinosaur animatronics scattered throughout, a large movie cinema, and a sensory maze where you have to find a way out through challenging obstacles, dark spaces, and illusions. They are also making an upcoming horror maze as well as an area for rock climbing. Another use is their large variety of foods they sell at their food court. Offering 21 different types of food stores, there is a shop to suit everyone’s tastes from burgers, bubble tea, sushi, and more.

The location of the SkyWorld centre is on 291-297 Queen Street, Auckland. Their contact number is 09 369 2405 can be used for any possible queries.

Snow Planet

As New Zealand’s only indoor snow entertainment centre, Snow Planet has several activities to participate in using their building that spans over 8000 square metres of snow. You can take a ride up the slope using their poma lift to a height that you are confident you are able to ski down with your skill level. If you are new to skiing or snowboarding, they have the option to do lessons. They also have a winter wonderland section where you can explore a small alpine village but also ride a tube or toboggan down two different super tubing tracks. When needing to take a break from the cold, they have a warm and cozy restaurant that comes complete with a view of the skiing slope as you enjoy your food and drinks.

Snow Planet is located at 91 Small Road, Silverdale, Auckland and can be reached through the number 0800 SNOWPLANET.

Paradice Ice Skating

Based in Avondale, Paradice ice skating has a large skating rink that is very spacious and can easily fit over 50 people simultaneously. Their prices are affordable and offer free skates to use along with the admission cost although better quality skates can be rented at an additional fee. Going ice skating at Paradice is not only a great way to have fun, it also gets the blood pumping. The experience is quite unique as you skate around the darkened rink with nice music playing in the background. There are also funky coloured lights that strobe every now and then so you can even dance around when going around. Even if you have no experience with skating whatsoever, it is easy to learn to get into the rhythm but if you still do not feel confident enough they have frames for all ages to help stay balanced. They often hold events of different kinds, play games, and have discounted prices at times which are great for when hanging out with friends since you can buy more food at their snack bar which sells all kinds of food and beverages from chicken nuggets to hot chocolate.

The address of Paradice ice skating is located at 150 Lansford Crescent, Avondale, Auckland and their phone number is 09 828 3800.

Megazone West

With an assortment to choose from, Megazone west has exciting activities such as laser tag, bumper cars, arcade machines, and virtual reality. Each laser tag round is fast paced as you skillfully move around the maze-like area in order to shoot other people’s vests and destroy their base. Their bumper car ride is a great way to enjoy yourself while releasing some stress as you drive around crashing, ramming, and smashing into other cars whether you do it on your own or along with a rider next to you. Inside their building, they also have a decent sized arcade with many types of games that you could enjoy while waiting for a round of laser tag to end, or something else. Right next to the entrance and counter they have virtual reality stations where you can play all kinds of different genre games while being completely immersed in the virtual experience. Megazone west also has deals that include the different activities they have so you can enjoy them at a cheaper price and have even more fun.

Megazone west is located at 3094 Great North Road, New Lynn, Auckland and can be contacted through 09 820 3900.

JUMP Avondale

If you are looking for a way to have fun while also doing some physical activity, Jump may be what you are looking for. As well as having a main court of 50 interconnected trampolines, they also have lots of other activities to choose from around their building. If you feel competitive you are able to battle others in an intense game of dodgeball that is refereed by their staff, or have a duel on an elevated beam over the foam pit using their soft jousting sticks in a battle of balance and power. Their foam pit that is filled with thousands of soft cubes also has a runway and launchpads as well as a two metre tall platform for those that are more daring. For those who have wanted to feel the joy of dunking, there are basketball lanes with height adjustable hoops so that no matter what age you are, everyone can enjoy it. If you are more of an active person, Jump has a performance wall where you are able to run up and do tricks, climbing sticks that while connected to a harness you can step from each one to the next and ascend higher as you go in order to gain confidence. They also have a parkour course filled with obstacles that you need to complete by swinging and leaping over. The parkour area is exclusive to this particular Jump centre and cannot be found elsewhere.

The Jump trampoline park is located at 6 Jomac Place, Avondale, Auckland and can be contacted via the number 0800 JUMP WITH US.
